# Machine Learning & AI for Robotics

Resources for introductory ML, reinforcement learning, and ML for perception — teaching robots to learn from data and experience.

## Resources

- **Machine Learning Specialization** — Andrew Ng, DeepLearning.AI & Stanford Online (Coursera, free to audit)
  https://www.coursera.org/specializations/machine-learning-introduction
  A beginner-friendly 3-course intro to supervised and unsupervised learning with hands-on Python (NumPy, scikit-learn, TensorFlow).

- **Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction (2nd ed.)** — Richard Sutton & Andrew Barto, MIT Press, 2018
  http://incompleteideas.net/book/the-book-2nd.html
  The foundational textbook of reinforcement learning, released free online under a Creative Commons license.

- **Spinning Up in Deep RL** — OpenAI
  https://spinningup.openai.com/en/latest/
  A free, self-contained educational resource with lightweight implementations and clear docs for learning deep RL algorithms.

- **Reinforcement Learning Course (UCL)** — David Silver, DeepMind/UCL
  https://www.davidsilver.uk/teaching/
  The classic free RL video-lecture series and slides, recommended as a first RL course.

- **Robotics: Perception** — Kostas Daniilidis & Jianbo Shi, University of Pennsylvania (Coursera, free to audit)
  https://www.coursera.org/learn/robotics-perception
  Teaches how robot-camera images become 3D information for grasping, visual odometry, and localization (camera models, pose estimation, multi-view geometry).

- **Robot Learning (CS 4756)** — Kuan Fang, Cornell University (course page, free)
  https://www.cs.cornell.edu/courses/cs4756/2026sp/
  Covers robot perception (state estimation, object detection, mapping) as probabilistic inference and robot decision-making (self-driving, manipulation, assistive robots) as Markov Decision Problems.

- **Robot Learning: From Fundamentals to Foundation Models** — Oier Mees, ETH Zurich (Computer Vision and Geometry Group; course page, free)
  https://cvg.ethz.ch/lectures/Robot-Learning/
  An advanced course spanning imitation learning, RL, and policy optimization through to Vision-Language-Action models, diffusion-based planning, transformers, world models, and generalist robot policies. Lecture slides and full YouTube recordings (including industry guest lectures from Google DeepMind, Meta, and NVIDIA) are freely available, along with a GitHub repo of coding assignments.

- **Practical Deep Learning for Coders** — fast.ai (Jeremy Howard)
  https://course.fast.ai/
  A strong applied ML on-ramp for perception tasks (also listed under Computer Vision).

## Notes

- Coursera courses are free to **audit** (full materials/videos), but graded assignments and certificates require payment or financial aid.
